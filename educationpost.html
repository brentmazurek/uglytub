General Care

First, do not touch the reglazed surface until the designated curing time. Do not open windows, or turn on air fans, as this may cause dirt and debris to fall into the fresh reglazing material, resulting in sharp points forming in the surface. If debris does fall into the tub, do not attampt to remove, as these will wear off over the first months use. Water drip bags have been placed over the water spout, to protect against water dripping onto the freshly reglazed surface. Should the bags begin to fill, the customer must replace the bags, or turn off the water flow. At the conclusion of the drying time, carefully remove any protective paper that was left, and discard. Replace the drain screen, and overflow stopper. Immediately recaulk around the DRAIN LIP, and the TUB RIM. Also, if it was not possible for our technicians to remove the overflow linkage, recaulk around the overflow plate also. Failure to apply and maintain a proper caulk seal may lead to water damage, and cause the reglazed material to peel. DO NOT USE SELF ADHESIVE CAULKING STRIPS.

Stains

To prevent stains from forming on your reglazed bathtub, do not use bleach, oils, or dyes. Avoid storing objects directly on the surface, especially metal cans which may form rust stains. If rust develops, attempt to remove with specialized cleaners such as Iron Out or Whink. Never hang garments, which could bleed stains, on your bathtub. DO NOT lay or store soap, sponges, cloths, bottles or any objects on the finish at any time! Purchase a shower rack instead.
