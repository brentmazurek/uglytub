Frequently Asked Questions

What exactly is bathtub reglazing?

Apply a new coating instead of purchasing a new tub!

Put simply, reglazing refers to the process of applying a new shiny coating on tubs, sinks, counters, and tile.

Do you use real porcelain?

We use a synthetic glaze that looks and feels like the original surface!

No one does. It is not possible to reapply porcelain. Porcelain is fired at approximately 1800 degrees, and therefore cannot be used to resurface your tub. In fact, if another company has stated that they will put the porcelain back on, we suggest you have the fire department standing by to put out the flames. We use a synthetic glaze that simulates the look and feel of the original surface.

What is the difference between the Standard and Deluxe finishes?

Both finishes provide you with an excellent looking bathtub!

Both are durable and chemically resistant materials. The urethane is an eco-friendly material, dries in 4 hours verses 48 for the epoxy, and can last 10 years. The epoxy does become brittle over time, which cuts it's average lifetime to 2-3 years.

What do I have to do to get ready for the reglazing?

Prep your bathroom for the optimal installation times!

Take your shower curtain or glass doors down. Clean the bathtub completely, and remove all personal items, such as hair curlers, tooth brushes, etc. Also, we require any leaking faucets to be repaired and not dripping. We also require working water and electricity, and the room must be at a normal temperature.

If I need to replace fixtures, walls, or floors in the bathroom, should I do them first?

Be sure to finish remodling before you refinish your tub!

Yes, it is a good idea to do all your work in the bathroom before we arrive. This eliminates the possibility of remodeling damage to the reglazed surface.

Are you insured?

Yes!

Yes, we are insured for 1 million dollars.

Can you fix chips and repair rust?

Have us check it out as each case varies!

Yes, in most cases. There is an extra charge for repairs, which is reasonably priced and always optional.

What about the odor?

The Deluxe finish is Eco-Friendly and avoids odor!

There is an odor to this process, but with our filtration system, the odor will be limited. The odor is not intolerable and will dissipate once cured.. Our Deluxe finish is an Eco-friendly finish.

Do you do any sandblasting of tubs?

No!

No, you will need to contact another company if your tub is in need of sandblasting.

Do you use any type of acid on the tubs?

We chemically etch the tub to ensure a proper bond!

In order to get the best bond between your tub and our material, our technicians will chemically etch the tubs to ensure bonding. There is very little smell, and the vapors are not harmful.

If my tub has been reglazed before, can it be reglazed again?

Yes, but we must strip the old coating!

Yes, it can be reglazed again. However, the old coating must be stripped off, and we charge an additional fee for this service. Generally, stripping a tub requires about 2 additional man hours.

If I drop something in my tub, will it chip?

Our touchup kits will help you protect your tub!

Your reglazed surface is both tough and durable. However, it is possible, just as it is with a new tub, to chip the surface. If this happens, promptly arrange for a service call, or use one of our touchup kits.

It was mentioned to me to wax the tub after it has dried for 90 days. What type do I use?

Wax your tub for the perfect shine!

Any good car wax can be used; after 90 days; to enhance the shine.

Why don't you warranty bathroom/kitchen sinks?

Due to the nature of sinks we cannot offer a warranty at this time!

We use the same material, same process and same quality workmanship as we do on our bathtubs, but we cannot warrant sinks due to the nature of their use. People tend to be more careless with sinks. Hot pots and pans are put in kitchen sinks, knives and forks are carelessly tossed, hot grease is poured on the surface and will ruin material, and drain cleaners are also used to the extreme. In bathroom sinks, women use hair dyes, drop hot curling irons and blow dryers, and drain cleanser.
